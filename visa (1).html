Indian visa services for UK residents

If you hold a British passport and don't have an OCI card, you need a visa every time you travel to India. The application process involves choosing the right visa type, completing an online form, preparing supporting documents (which vary by visa type), and attending a VFS Global centre appointment.

Sorted takes the complexity out of this process. We help you choose the right visa type, complete the online application, prepare all supporting documents including invitation letters and financial statements, and guide you through your VFS appointment. All for £150.

Visa types we handle

  • Tourist visa — For holiday and family visits to India. Available in 6-month, 1-year, and 5-year multi-entry options
  • Visiting visa for parents/family — For Indian family members visiting you in the UK. Includes invitation letter preparation, financial proof, and accommodation evidence
  • Business visa — For conferences, meetings, and business travel to India. Requires company letters and Indian organisation invitation
  • E-visa (electronic visa) — Quick electronic visa for short trips. Valid for 60 days with double entry. Processed in 48–72 hours
  • Child/minor visa — Special requirements for British-born children travelling to India. Requires consent forms from both parents
  • 5-year multi-entry visa — Best value for frequent travellers to India. Apply once, travel freely for five years

Visa vs OCI — consider the long-term option

If you're of Indian origin and plan to visit India regularly, an OCI card (£175 through Sorted + ~£215 government fees) gives you lifetime unlimited access to India with no visa needed ever again. Compare that with renewing a tourist visa every 1–5 years. For many NRIs, OCI is the smarter long-term investment. Sorted can advise on which option makes more sense for your situation.

Visa FAQ

Common Indian visa questions

How do I get a visiting visa for my parents from India?
Your parents apply through the Indian visa portal. They need an invitation letter from you (we draft this), your UK address proof, financial evidence showing you can support their stay, accommodation details, and travel insurance. Sorted prepares the complete application package for £150.
What is the difference between an Indian e-visa and regular visa?
An e-visa is quick to get (48–72 hours, no VFS visit needed) but only valid for 60 days with double entry. A regular sticker visa can last up to 5 years with unlimited entries. For frequent travellers, the regular visa is far better value. Sorted helps you choose the right option.
Can I get a 5-year Indian tourist visa from the UK?
Yes. The 5-year multiple entry tourist visa is available for UK passport holders. It's the best option if you visit India regularly — apply once, travel freely for five years. Government fee is approximately £100.
Does my British-born child need a visa for India?
Yes, if your child holds a British passport and doesn't have an OCI card, they need a visa. Children's applications require consent forms from both parents. Alternatively, consider an OCI card instead — it's a one-time lifetime visa that avoids needing to apply for visas ever again.
How do I apply for an Indian business visa from UK?
Business visas need an invitation letter from the Indian organisation, a letter from your UK employer confirming the business purpose, and details of your travel itinerary. Sorted drafts all required letters and prepares the complete application.
How much does an Indian visa cost from UK?
Sorted charges £150 for complete application assistance including invitation letter drafting. Government visa fees vary: tourist e-visas from ~£25, regular tourist visas from ~£80, 5-year visas from ~£100. VFS service charges of ~£15–25 are additional. Total range: £190–£275 depending on visa type.
Should I get a visa or an OCI card?
If you're of Indian origin and plan to visit India regularly, an OCI card gives you lifetime unlimited access with no visa needed ever again. It's a one-time investment vs recurring visa costs. Sorted can advise on which makes more sense for your situation — WhatsApp us to discuss.
