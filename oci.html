What is an OCI card?

The Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) card is essentially a lifetime visa to India. It gives you multiple-entry, multi-purpose, lifelong access to India with no restrictions on the duration of stay. If you've become a British citizen but want to maintain your connection to India — for family visits, property ownership, or business — the OCI card is what you need.

Unlike a tourist visa which expires and needs renewing, the OCI card is a one-time application that lasts forever. It's the closest thing to dual citizenship that India offers, since India does not permit dual nationality.

Who can apply for OCI from the UK?

You're eligible for OCI if you fall into any of these categories:

  • Former Indian citizens who have become British citizens (the most common scenario)
  • Children of Indian citizens or former Indian citizens, including British-born children where at least one parent is of Indian origin
  • Spouses of Indian citizens or OCI holders, regardless of nationality
  • PIO card holders who need to convert their old PIO to OCI (mandatory since 2015)
  • Grandchildren and great-grandchildren of Indian citizens (with some conditions)

OCI FAQ

Frequently asked questions about OCI

How do I apply for an OCI card in the UK?
You apply online at ociservices.gov.in, upload documents and photos, then visit a VFS Global centre with originals. Sorted handles the entire online process for £175 — we complete the form, prepare your documents, resize your photos to exact 51×51mm specifications, and guide you through the VFS appointment.
How long does OCI take from UK?
Typically 6–8 weeks from VFS submission to receiving your card by post. A cleanly submitted application avoids queries that cause delays. Sorted's 98% first-time approval rate means your application is processed without unnecessary hold-ups.
Can my British-born child get an OCI card?
Yes, if at least one parent is of Indian origin. The child needs a British passport first. We recommend completing Form I (birth registration) before applying for OCI, though they can be done in parallel in some cases.
What is the difference between OCI and PIO?
PIO (Person of Indian Origin) cards were merged into OCI in 2015 by the Indian government. If you still have a PIO card, it must be converted to OCI. Sorted handles this conversion as part of our standard service for £175.
Do I need to re-issue my OCI after getting a new passport?
Yes. When you get a new passport, your OCI must be re-issued to link to the new passport number. This is especially important for children under 20 who renew passports frequently. Failure to update can cause problems at Indian immigration and even denied boarding.
How much does OCI cost in the UK?
Sorted charges £175 for the complete service. VFS Global charges approximately £215 as government and processing fees, payable at your VFS appointment. Total: approximately £390.
Can a spouse of an Indian citizen apply for OCI?
Yes. Spouses of Indian citizens or OCI holders are eligible. Additional documents include marriage certificate and spouse's Indian passport or OCI card. Sorted handles spouse-based applications regularly.
I surrendered my Indian passport years ago — can I still get OCI?
Yes, as long as you have your surrender certificate. If you've lost the surrender certificate, you'll need to apply for a duplicate first. Sorted can guide you through this entire process including obtaining a duplicate surrender certificate.
What happens if my OCI application gets a query?
A "query" means the processing centre found an issue with your application and is sending it back for correction. Common reasons include photo spec errors, document mismatches, and incomplete information. If this happens with a Sorted application, we handle the resubmission at no extra cost — but with our 98% first-time approval rate, queries are very rare.
Can I travel to India while my OCI application is being processed?
Yes, but you'll need a separate tourist visa since your OCI isn't active yet. If you have an existing valid OCI that's being re-issued, you should carry both your old OCI booklet and the VFS receipt as proof. Sorted can advise on your specific situation.
