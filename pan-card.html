Why do NRIs need a PAN card?

A Permanent Account Number (PAN) is essential for almost any financial transaction in India. If you're an NRI, OCI holder, or British citizen with financial interests in India, you'll need a PAN card for:

  • Opening or maintaining Indian bank accounts (NRE, NRO, savings, fixed deposits)
  • Buying or selling property in India — PAN is mandatory for registration
  • Filing Indian income tax returns on rental income, investments, or capital gains
  • Investing in mutual funds, shares, or bonds in India
  • Lower TDS rates — without PAN, withholding tax on Indian income is charged at higher rates

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a notary or solicitor for PAN application?

No — with our service, no notary or solicitor attestation is required. We handle the application so you can submit directly by post.

Which form do I need — 49A or 49AA?

If you hold an Indian passport (NRI), you need Form 49A. If you're a British citizen, OCI holder, or other foreign national, you need Form 49AA. We'll determine the right one for you.

How long does it take to get my PAN card?
